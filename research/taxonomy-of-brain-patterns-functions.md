# Taxonomy of Brain Patterns & Functions

A comprehensive mapping of cognitive processes and neural systems.

---

## üß† 1. Cognitive Control & Executive Functions

**Primary Region:** Prefrontal Cortex

- **Thinking:** Conscious reasoning, weighing evidence, making judgments
- **Problem Solving:** Generating, evaluating, and implementing solutions
- **Planning:** Sequencing steps toward goals
- **Decision Making:** Balancing reward, risk, and timing
- **Working Memory:** Holding and manipulating short-term information
- **Inhibition:** Resisting impulses, distractions, or habitual responses
- **Cognitive Flexibility:** Shifting between tasks or mental frameworks

## üí≠ 2. Memory Systems

**Primary Regions:** Hippocampus, prefrontal cortex, and temporal lobes

- **Short-Term / Working Memory:** Temporary mental workspace
- **Long-Term Memory:** Stable storage (facts, skills, experiences)
- **Episodic Memory:** Personal events and experiences
- **Semantic Memory:** General knowledge and concepts
- **Procedural Memory:** Learned skills and habits
- **Emotional Memory:** Linking events with affective states (amygdala-driven)

## üí° 3. Creativity & Imagination

**Primary Regions:** Default Mode Network, Executive Control Network, and Salience Network

- **Divergent Thinking:** Generating multiple ideas or solutions
- **Convergent Thinking:** Evaluating and narrowing to the best answer
- **Insight / "Aha!" Moments:** Sudden neural reconfiguration of ideas
- **Imagination:** Mental simulation beyond direct experience
- **Visualization:** Constructing internal images or scenarios

## ‚ö° 4. Emotional & Motivational Systems

**Primary Regions:** Limbic system (amygdala, nucleus accumbens, hypothalamus)

- **Emotion Processing:** Interpreting and expressing feelings
- **Reward Learning:** Driven by dopamine signaling (motivation, curiosity)
- **Fear Conditioning:** Associating stimuli with threats (survival mechanism)
- **Empathy:** Mirroring and understanding others' states (insula, mirror neurons)
- **Mood Regulation:** Long-term modulation via serotonin, dopamine, and cortisol balance

## üîÅ 5. Learning & Adaptation

**Concept:** The brain as a self-rewriting algorithm

- **Classical Conditioning:** Linking two stimuli (Pavlov-style)
- **Operant Conditioning:** Learning via reward/punishment feedback loops
- **Hebbian Learning:** "Neurons that fire together, wire together"
- **Neuroplasticity:** Structural rewiring through practice or trauma recovery
- **Metacognition:** Thinking about your own thinking (reflective awareness)

## üåê 6. Perception & Sensory Integration

**Process:** Raw sensory input transformed into coherent experience

- **Visual Processing:** Occipital lobe integration of light and shape
- **Auditory Processing:** Temporal lobe decoding of pitch, rhythm, and language
- **Somatosensory Integration:** Tactile and proprioceptive awareness
- **Multisensory Binding:** Merging visual, auditory, and spatial data into one scene
- **Prediction & Error Correction:** The brain's constant Bayesian modeling of reality

## üó£Ô∏è 7. Language & Symbolic Thought

**Primary Dominance:** Left hemisphere

- **Comprehension:** Wernicke's area for understanding meaning
- **Production:** Broca's area for speech and syntax
- **Inner Speech:** Silent verbal thought for planning and self-regulation
- **Metaphor & Abstraction:** Higher-order symbolic mapping

## üßò 8. Rest, Insight, and Reflection Networks

**Primary Region:** Default Mode Network and associated cortical hubs

- **Mind Wandering:** Spontaneous associative processing
- **Dreaming:** Unconstrained narrative generation for memory integration
- **Intuition:** Unconscious pattern recognition
- **Insight Generation:** Cross-linking disparate neural representations

## ‚öôÔ∏è 9. Social & Interpersonal Cognition

**Concept:** The brain as a social prediction engine

- **Theory of Mind:** Modeling others' beliefs and intentions
- **Social Reward / Belonging:** Mediated by oxytocin and dopamine
- **Group Dynamics:** Conformity, leadership, cooperation
- **Moral Reasoning:** Balancing fairness, empathy, and self-interest

## üß© 10. Homeostasis & Self-Regulation

**Primary Regions:** Subcortical systems maintaining survival and balance

- **Autonomic Control:** Heart rate, breathing, temperature
- **Hunger / Thirst Regulation:** Hypothalamic feedback loops
- **Sleep-Wake Regulation:** Circadian rhythm synchronization
- **Stress Response:** Hypothalamic-pituitary-adrenal (HPA) axis and cortisol cycles

## üîÆ Summary

The brain operates as a **layered adaptive system**:

- **Cortex:** Conscious reasoning and planning
- **Limbic System:** Emotion and motivation
- **Basal Ganglia:** Habit and action gating
- **Cerebellum:** Coordination, prediction, and fine-tuning
- **Brainstem:** Survival automation

Together, they form a hybrid of computation, chemistry, and prediction ‚Äî constantly optimizing for survival, novelty, and meaning.
